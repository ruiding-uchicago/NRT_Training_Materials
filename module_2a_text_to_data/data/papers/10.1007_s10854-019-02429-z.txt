Poly(vinyl alcohol) (PVA) as a conventional polymer dielectric material which possesses excellent insulating performance by cross-linking of the hydroxyl-rich group (–OH) has been utilized extensively in organic thin-film transistors (OTFTs). In this work, a novel strategy for OTFTs, constructing with tandem dielectric films, is studied. The air stability of OTFTs can be conveniently modulated by the construction of tandem dielectrics. Herein, with the optimization for the dielectric structures and interface engineering, a holistic understanding of the hydroxyl group for the performance of OTFTs and OTFT-based gas sensors is provided. Implementing the interface properties affords double dielectric OTFTs that deliver a remarkable responsivity for 10 ppm NO2nearly 62% and triple dielectric OTFTs that maintain a slight fluctuation in their electrical performance during 10-day atmospheric storage, respectively. The enhancement of air stability of OTFTs and sensing performance for NO2can be attributed to the passivation controlling effect of PVA, and these results demonstrate that the simple and effective method provides new insight into the design of functional groups of organic dielectric materials for high-performance, air-stable OTFTs and high responsivity for OTFT-based gas sensors.

Nitrogen dioxide (NO2), as a typical air pollutant resulting from fossil fuels for industrial productions and transportations, is reddish brown and one of the toxic gases with irritating smell [1]. As NO2is prone to endanger public health and even low concentrations of NO2atmosphere (~ 1 ppm) could induce adverse pulmonary effects like chronic bronchitis, pediatric asthma and emphysema [2], it is essential to realize the precise and efficient detection of NO2for human health and disease prevention. In the past decade, many efforts have been put forth to develop a high responsivity, fast response, low-cost sensor and simple fabrication process [3,4]. Currently, several technologies, including electrochemical, resistive, electrical and optical type sensing, are widely used for NO2detection [5]. Among these gas sensors, simple, readily manufactured sensors based on organic thin-film transistors (OTFTs) have more potential value in the field of gas sensing in terms of low cost, low power consumption and high performance, which are regarded as prospective candidates for next-generation gas sensors. Nowadays, OTFTs have attracted a huge research interest as significant components in biocompatible/flexible electronic devices [6,7,8]. There are many efforts intensively made on the standpoints of advances in materials, fabrication complexity and new applications such as integrated circuits [9], radio-frequency identification (RFID) tags [10] and chemical/biological sensors [11], wherein dielectric materials serve as an indispensable part for the targeted electronics. Compared with the conventional inorganic dielectric like SiO2, polymer dielectrics have been regarded as one of the most promising dielectric materials with their excellent insulating performance and mechanical flexibility suitable for flexible/wearable electronic devices [12,13,14,15]. Moreover, the intrinsic chemical characteristics (molecular weight, polarity, solubility, chain relaxation, etc.) of polymer dielectrics can be readily tuned by the design of monomeric precursors and polymerizing conditions [16,17]. To date, a variety of polymer dielectrics such as poly(methyl methacrylate) (PMMA), poly(vinyl alcohol) (PVA), poly(4-vinyl phenol) (PVP), polyimide (PI) and polystyrene (PS) have been widely applied in OTFT-based organic electronics [18,19,20,21,22,23]. However, as one grave drawback of most OTFTs described in the literature is the lack of stability of devices under ambient conditions, many efforts have been made for the stability of OTFT devices.

To enhance OTFT devices’ reliability of work performance, typical approaches have focused on the organic semiconductors (OSCs) and dielectric materials [24,25,26]. Dai et al. developed OTFTs with reliability factors exceeding 90% using oriented flow and crystallization of OSC in the titled spin-coating process, which can form a well-ordered molecular arrangement and layer-by-layer structures in OSC films [27]. Another strategy focused on the doping of a weak channel for better contact injection in the OSC film [28]. Henning et al. used molecular additives to displace nanometer-sized voids in the polymer microstructure, improving the stability and uniformity for industrial applications and showing the physical mechanisms behind operational and environmental degradation for OTFT devices [29]. Synthesis of high-permittivity (k) dielectric materials and interfacial optimizations were also adopted for realizing high performance and stability of OTFT-based devices [30,31,32]. For instance, the design of novel structural aromatic polyimides as gate dielectrics with efficient interface engineering strategies can facilitate to develop lightweight, flexibility and excellent thermal stability organic electronic devices [33]. From these results, it can be surmised that well-crystallized OSCs, low-trap-density OSC/dielectric interface and high-k gate dielectrics are required for the stability of high-performance OTFT sensors, which may introduce complexity and higher fabrication cost. However, the influence of the hydroxyl-rich functional group (–OH) in dielectric on performance and stability in OTFTs is still lack of research. Herein, we devised a simple and effective tandem dielectric method which was constructed by the conventional polymeric materials (PMMA, PVA). The fundamental relationship of a hydroxyl group in dielectric films corresponding to the OTFT performance was further studied, and this phenomenon was applied to the OTFT-based gas sensors.

In this work, we investigated the influence of hydroxyl group in dielectrics on the environmental stability as well as the uniformity of mobility OTFTs, and systematically optimized and characterized several representative OSCs of sensing properties in OTFT-based gas sensors. As a result, we realized stable high-performance OTFT-based gas sensors, which were implemented by simple hydrogen bond passivation treatment of the gate dielectrics. In the construction of the triple-layer dielectric system, we utilized the OTFTs consisting of α-sexithiophene (α-6T) and copper phthalocyanine (CuPc) as the organic semiconductor layer for comparison, elaborately illuminating the van der Waals forces and other complicated interactions in dielectric layers during the degradation of OTFTs, and then, we surmised the passivation mechanism for OTFTs in their long-term environmental stability.

Figure1shows the architecture of bottom-gate top-contact OTFTs, indicated here as TFT-n (n= 1–3), where the monolayer PMMA film, bilayer PMMA/PVA film and multilayer PMMA/PVA/PMMA film act as the gate insulator. All the OTFTs were fabricated on the patterned indium tin oxide (ITO)-coated glass substrate, which acted as the gate electrode. After the substrates were routinely cleaned, PVA (Mw ~ 146 k–186 kDa) and PMMA (average Mw ~ 120 kDa) were dissolved in deionized water (40 mg/mL) and anisole (50 mg/mL and 100 mg/mL), respectively. PMMA (50 mg/mL) was spin-coated on the ITO substrate at 2000 rpm for 60 s (~ 150 nm), and PVA was spin-coated on PMMA dielectric layer at 3000 rpm for 60 s (~ 250 nm). Subsequently, the PMMA (50 mg/mL) on the top layer was spin-coated at 2000 rpm for 60 s (~ 150 nm). Each layer was baked in an oven at 100 °C for 1 h after coating. For comparison, a single dielectric layer of PMMA (100 mg/mL) was spin-coated on the ITO substrate at 1500 rpm for 60 s (~ 510 nm) as the monolayer PMMA film. After the preparation of gate dielectric layers, the organic semiconductor (OSC) thin films evaporated thermally under the pressure 2 × 10−4Pa at a rate of 0.2–0.3 Å s−1. All the materials were purchased from Sigma-Aldrich and used without further purification. Finally, the source and drain electrodes of 50 nm gold (Au) were thermally deposited on the OSC film into well-defined channels through metal shadow masks. The length and width of channels were 100 μm and 1 cm, respectively.

From theI–Vcharacteristics, the mobility (μ) and subthreshold swing (SS) of TFT-n devices with the storage time are shown in Figs.2b and3a. These OTFT data indicate the obvious difference of air stability between TFT-n devices. As can be noted, theμ’s of TFT-1 and TFT-3 devices merely has a slight decrease during the 10-day storage in ambient air, while theμof TFT-2 devices decrease from 0.100 cm2V−1S−1(fresh devices) to 0.048 cm2V−1S−1(after 10 days) emerging drastic fluctuations, with the performance degradation near 52%. Besides, as shown in Fig.3a, it is noteworthy that the TFT-2 devices possess more drastic changes during the first-day storage environment and always keep a much highSS(up to 160.4%) in the following days. As the trap density (N) is proportional toSS, which could be extracted by Eq. (3), these data support that the TFT-2 devices generate higher trap density during the same storage circumstance compared with the other two devices. Thus, we surmise the effects of H bonds in a dielectric layer over time have a significant impact on the performance of OTFTs.

aVariation ofSSof TFT-n devices with storage time. (b–d) FT-IR spectroscopy of three types of dielectrics for fresh and storing 5 days in air.eSchematic illustration for the change of H bond conformation in TFT-2 (left) and TFT-3 (right) during the same storage process

To study the essential relationship between the H bonds in dielectrics and trap density under storage time, the dielectrics of TFT-n devices were further investigated using Fourier transform infrared (FT-IR) measurements. We utilized the quartz glass substrates to substitute the ITO substrates, minimizing noise and retaining the surface characteristics of dielectrics, and the FT-IR spectrum was plotted by the same coordinate system. As shown in Fig.3b–d, all the samples show R-CH3asymmetric stretching vibrations in 2944–2951 cm−1and the double-bond C=O bending vibration at 1727–1731 cm−1, which are the typical absorption bands for PMMA. The FT-IR spectrum for TFT-2 and TFT-3 samples which contain the PVA film shows an O–H stretching mode at 3314–3316 cm−1, indicating the presence of H–bond in these films. Importantly, the signal of TFT-2 increase could be observed at 3314.17 cm−1with the change of storage time, while the intensity of TFT-3 decreases. Thus, the combined FT-IR experiments demonstrate that the TFT-2 devices could generate stronger van der Waals forces between H–bonds and gas molecules when placed a long time in the air, and another passivation layer PMMA would be instrumental in eliminating this phenomenon and then strengthen the air stability for OTFT devices (Fig.3e).

The representative transfer curves of TFT-n α-6T devices exposed to varying NO2are shown in Fig.4a, and the transfer curves were measured under aVDSof − 40 V. Upon NO2exposure, theIONof all TFT-n devices have an increasing trend, along with an enlargedIOFF, and the highest responsivity could be seen in the TFT-2 device (R≈ 62.08%), which is nearly 3.6 times of the responsivity of TFT-3 device (R≈ 17.10%) under 10 ppm NO2. The difference in gas sensitivity could be attributed to the dipole moment of the hydroxyl group (–OH) in PVA film which has a stronger attraction to NO2molecules, while the PMMA film passivated this process in TFT-3 device [35,36].

Notably, the trap density increases with the concentration of NO2and more mobile holes are generated in the OSC channel. This phenomenon could also be observed in the exposure of the TFT-n device in the air after five days (Fig.4b). Although all of these had a slight decrease inION, the responsivity for 10 ppm NO2of TFT-2 device (R≈ 58.17%) and TFT-3 device (R≈ 26.56%) remains in a rational fluctuation range. These results demonstrate that the variation of trap density caused by the hydroxyl group (–OH) in PVA film directly contributes to the distinct responsivity for the detection of NO2analyte.

To confirm the essential reason for the variations of TFT-n in sensing performance, α-6T films fabricated by the same day were investigated using atomic force microscopy (AFM), and the morphologies of OSC films are shown in Fig.5. Figure5a–c shows the AFM of the same TFT-n devices after storage certain days respectively, which could help to understand the relationship between the mechanism of gas sensing and degradation of devices performance to some extent. Notably, α-6T films deposited on the above dielectrics still exhibit similar crystal growth (Fig.5a). On the other hand, it could be observed that the molecular orientation of α-6T films varied with the increase in storage time, indicating that the in-plane anisotropic orientation is gradually tended on the dielectric surface with the storage in air (Fig.5b, c) [40]. Thus, we can assume that the distinct sensing property has less related to the size of grain or morphologies of OSC film, and the absorption of NO2analyte at the dielectrics and the interface between dielectric/OSC layers is the primary reason for the diverse sensing performance of the TFT-n devices.

In summary, we systemically investigated the intrinsic characteristics of OTFT performance based on device structures and then surmised the mechanism of gas sensing in which gas analyte interacted with the functional group (–OH) in dielectrics during the gas detection process. The proposed TFT-2 device with high field-effect mobility of 0.085 cm2V−1s−1and higher responsivity of 62% for 10 ppm NO2and the TFT-3 device with improved air stability were both realized by the comprehension for the OTFT structures. As a result, the TFT-2 device could achieve higher signal-to-noise ratio, lower requirements for highly integrated gas sensing matrix or back-end circuits with its remarkable field-effect mobility, while the TFT-3 could endure worse atmosphere and raise the life span of OTFT-based gas sensors. Furthermore, as the improved performance (whether the field-effect mobility or the responsivity to the gas analyte) results from device structures rather than the selection of particular OSC materials, we await that the simple but effective method can be also carried out with other material combinations for better device fabrication and application.

AbstractPoly(vinyl alcohol) (PVA) as a conventional polymer dielectric material which possesses excellent insulating performance by cross-linking of the hydroxyl-rich group (–OH) has been utilized extensively in organic thin-film transistors (OTFTs). In this work, a novel strategy for OTFTs, constructing with tandem dielectric films, is studied. The air stability of OTFTs can be conveniently modulated by the construction of tandem dielectrics. Herein, with the optimization for the dielectric structures and interface engineering, a holistic understanding of the hydroxyl group for the performance of OTFTs and OTFT-based gas sensors is provided. Implementing the interface properties affords double dielectric OTFTs that deliver a remarkable responsivity for 10 ppm NO2nearly 62% and triple dielectric OTFTs that maintain a slight fluctuation in their electrical performance during 10-day atmospheric storage, respectively. As NO2is prone to endanger public health and even low concentrations of NO2atmosphere (~ 1 ppm) could induce adverse pulmonary effects like chronic bronchitis, pediatric asthma and emphysema [2], it is essential to realize the precise and efficient detection of NO2for human health and disease prevention. In the past decade, many efforts have been put forth to develop a high responsivity, fast response, low-cost sensor and simple fabrication process [3,4]. Currently, several technologies, including electrochemical, resistive, electrical and optical type sensing, are widely used for NO2detection [5]. Among these gas sensors, simple, readily manufactured sensors based on organic thin-film transistors (OTFTs) have more potential value in the field of gas sensing in terms of low cost, low power consumption and high performance, which are regarded as prospective candidates for next-generation gas sensors. Nowadays, OTFTs have attracted a huge research interest as significant components in biocompatible/flexible electronic devices [6,7,8]. There are many efforts intensively made on the standpoints of advances in materials, fabrication complexity and new applications such as integrated circuits [9], radio-frequency identification (RFID) tags [10] and chemical/biological sensors [11], wherein dielectric materials serve as an indispensable part for the targeted electronics. Compared with the conventional inorganic dielectric like SiO2, polymer dielectrics have been regarded as one of the most promising dielectric materials with their excellent insulating performance and mechanical flexibility suitable for flexible/wearable electronic devices [12,13,14,15]. Moreover, the intrinsic chemical characteristics (molecular weight, polarity, solubility, chain relaxation, etc.) of polymer dielectrics can be readily tuned by the design of monomeric precursors and polymerizing conditions [16,17]. To date, a variety of polymer dielectrics such as poly(methyl methacrylate) (PMMA), poly(vinyl alcohol) (PVA), poly(4-vinyl phenol) (PVP), polyimide (PI) and polystyrene (PS) have been widely applied in OTFT-based organic electronics [18,19,20,21,22,23]. However, as one grave drawback of most OTFTs described in the literature is the lack of stability of devices under ambient conditions, many efforts have been made for the stability of OTFT devices.To enhance OTFT devices’ reliability of work performance, typical approaches have focused on the organic semiconductors (OSCs) and dielectric materials [24,25,26]. Dai et al. developed OTFTs with reliability factors exceeding 90% using oriented flow and crystallization of OSC in the titled spin-coating process, which can form a well-ordered molecular arrangement and layer-by-layer structures in OSC films [27]. Another strategy focused on the doping of a weak channel for better contact injection in the OSC film [28]. Henning et al. used molecular additives to displace nanometer-sized voids in the polymer microstructure, improving the stability and uniformity for industrial applications and showing the physical mechanisms behind operational and environmental degradation for OTFT devices [29]. Synthesis of high-permittivity (k) dielectric materials and interfacial optimizations were also adopted for realizing high performance and stability of OTFT-based devices [30,31,32]. For instance, the design of novel structural aromatic polyimides as gate dielectrics with efficient interface engineering strategies can facilitate to develop lightweight, flexibility and excellent thermal stability organic electronic devices [33]. From these results, it can be surmised that well-crystallized OSCs, low-trap-density OSC/dielectric interface and high-k gate dielectrics are required for the stability of high-performance OTFT sensors, which may introduce complexity and higher fabrication cost. However, the influence of the hydroxyl-rich functional group (–OH) in dielectric on performance and stability in OTFTs is still lack of research. Herein, we devised a simple and effective tandem dielectric method which was constructed by the conventional polymeric materials (PMMA, PVA). The fundamental relationship of a hydroxyl group in dielectric films corresponding to the OTFT performance was further studied, and this phenomenon was applied to the OTFT-based gas sensors.In this work, we investigated the influence of hydroxyl group in dielectrics on the environmental stability as well as the uniformity of mobility OTFTs, and systematically optimized and characterized several representative OSCs of sensing properties in OTFT-based gas sensors. As a result, we realized stable high-performance OTFT-based gas sensors, which were implemented by simple hydrogen bond passivation treatment of the gate dielectrics. In the construction of the triple-layer dielectric system, we utilized the OTFTs consisting of α-sexithiophene (α-6T) and copper phthalocyanine (CuPc) as the organic semiconductor layer for comparison, elaborately illuminating the van der Waals forces and other complicated interactions in dielectric layers during the degradation of OTFTs, and then, we surmised the passivation mechanism for OTFTs in their long-term environmental stability.2Experimental procedureFigure1shows the architecture of bottom-gate top-contact OTFTs, indicated here as TFT-n (n= 1–3), where the monolayer PMMA film, bilayer PMMA/PVA film and multilayer PMMA/PVA/PMMA film act as the gate insulator. All the OTFTs were fabricated on the patterned indium tin oxide (ITO)-coated glass substrate, which acted as the gate electrode. After the substrates were routinely cleaned, PVA (Mw ~ 146 k–186 kDa) and PMMA (average Mw ~ 120 kDa) were dissolved in deionized water (40 mg/mL) and anisole (50 mg/mL and 100 mg/mL), respectively. PMMA (50 mg/mL) was spin-coated on the ITO substrate at 2000 rpm for 60 s (~ 150 nm), and PVA was spin-coated on PMMA dielectric layer at 3000 rpm for 60 s (~ 250 nm). Subsequently, the PMMA (50 mg/mL) on the top layer was spin-coated at 2000 rpm for 60 s (~ 150 nm). Each layer was baked in an oven at 100 °C for 1 h after coating. For comparison, a single dielectric layer of PMMA (100 mg/mL) was spin-coated on the ITO substrate at 1500 rpm for 60 s (~ 510 nm) as the monolayer PMMA film. After the preparation of gate dielectric layers, the organic semiconductor (OSC) thin films evaporated thermally under the pressure 2 × 10−4Pa at a rate of 0.2–0.3 Å s−1. All the materials were purchased from Sigma-Aldrich and used without further purification. Finally, the source and drain electrodes of 50 nm gold (Au) were thermally deposited on the OSC film into well-defined channels through metal shadow masks. The proposed TFT-2 device with high field-effect mobility of 0.085 cm2V−1s−1and higher responsivity of 62% for 10 ppm NO2and the TFT-3 device with improved air stability were both realized by the comprehension for the OTFT structures. As a result, the TFT-2 device could achieve higher signal-to-noise ratio, lower requirements for highly integrated gas sensing matrix or back-end circuits with its remarkable field-effect mobility, while the TFT-3 could endure worse atmosphere and raise the life span of OTFT-based gas sensors.
