Abstract‚ÄîAimed at the dual biofunctional membrane comprised
with carrier layer and sensing layer used in BioFET(Bio Field
Effect Transistor) nowadays, this paper designed a kind of novel
one-layer membrane comprising polypyrrole and urease with
electrochemical method, which can replace traditional doublelayer membrane, and give a new method for the design of the
functional membrane of BioFET. The BioFET sensor show a
linearity of 0.9995 in the urea concentration range of 10-7
mol/L~10-3mol/L, and a high sensitivity of 45.9mV/pUrea was
obtained.
Keywords- Polypyrrole; Biofunctional membrane; BioFET
I. INTRODUCTION
With the micro-processing technology to mature, the
sensor is moving in small, integrated, low-power direction.
ISFETs (Ion Sensitive Field Electric Transistor) originated
from MOSFET, have the inherent advantages of small size, low
power consumption and easy to be integrated, received
extensive attention in the past two decades, and has been
rapidly developed.
Applications of ISFET are extended from the previous
detection of inorganic cations to the current organic ions,
pesticides, gas molecules, DNA, biological molecules and
other substances. Accordingly, the sensitive materials
developed from inorganic materials, such as SiO2„ÄÅSi3N4„ÄÅ
Al2O3„ÄÅTa2O5, to organic materials, such as PPy, PAn, PVA,
etc al. In the aspects of bio-moleculars detection,
ImmunoFET[1], EnFET[2], CellFET[3], and other kinds of
BioFETs[4-6] have been developed. Detection of the enzyme
which is more current research and application of a class, such
as urease sensor, glucose oxidase sensor, et, al. Whereas, the
biological sensitive membrane reported in the articles, is
made of double layers, namely carrier layer and the sensitive
layer, and they are independent. This paper described a new
kind of BioFET with one layer which breaks through
traditional structures, and has the same function as the double
layers mentioned above.
II. PREPARATION OF THE SENSITIVE MEMBRAE WITH PPY
MATERIAL
A. Reagents and Instruments
Urease was purchased from MERCK Company; urea and
pyrrole was purchased from Sigma Company; Na2HPO4 and
NaH2PO4 were purchased from Beijing Chemical Reagent
Company; solvent, deionized water; the MEC-12B Multifunction computer electrochemical analyzer used for the
electrochemical polymerization of the instrument purchased
from Jiangsu Jiangfen Electroanalytical Co., Ltd.
B. Pyrrole solution preparation
Deionized water as solvent, Na2HPO4 and NaH2PO4 as
electrolyte and used to adjust the solution pH. Since the
optimum pH value of urease is 7.0 to 7.5, so the pyrrole
solution was prepared with the following steps: firstly,
preparing the phosphate buffer with concentration of 0.1mol / L
and pH value of 7.4; secondly, then adding pyrrole monomer
(previously distilled); finally, pyrrole solution with
concentration of 0.1mol/L was obtained and stored in dark
place and 4 ‚ÑÉ condition.
C. Urease-Pyrrole solution preparation
Deionized water as solvent, preparing urease solution with
the concentration of 20mg/mL; taking the previously prepared
pyrrole solution 9mL, and adding 1mL urease solution into it,
then the urease-pyrrole solution was obtained.
D. Biosensitive membrane preparation
In this study the method of two-electrode system and cyclic
voltammetry was adopt to preparing biosensitive film. In
particular: ISFET gate area as the working electrode, platinum
wire as the electrode and reference electrode, and PPy / Urease
film growing onto the ISFET gate area. In the preliminary
study, we found that good quality Polyprrole films can be
obtained with the following preparing parameters: voltage of -
0.05 ~ 0.75V, scan rate at 50mV / s, temperature at 4 ‚ÑÉ, the

To obtain the sensor performance, urea solution with
suitable parameters is necessary. The buffer concentration and
pH value has great affect on the sensor performance, such as
response reproducibility, response time, calibration curve slope,
etc. The higher buffer concentration, the stronger the buffering
capacity, and the smaller the pH value changes near the
enzyme membrane affected by the reaction products.
Accordingly, the response sensitivity is lower. On the other
hand, because of the small changes of pH value, the range of
enzyme kinetics becomes wide. And the up limit of calibration
curve linearity is high under the condition of high buffer
concentration. Otherwise, the response sensitivity is high and
the up limit of the linearity range is low, when the buffer
concentration is low.
As the enzyme kinetics is relative to the pH value of the
reaction system, and the pH value affects buffer capacity, so,
performances of the sensor response depend on pH value.
In summary analysis, the urea solution obtained by the
method in this experiment is suitable.
The results of the sensor sensitivity got by above method
show that Ppy membrane obtained by cyclic voltammetry has
good response to pH value, and it provides the basis for further
detection of urea.

V. CONLUSION
Traditional preparation methods for biosensitive membrane
allow the detection of urea, but the process is very complicated,
and difficult to guarantee consistency. The work of this paper
shows that use of the special properties of organic compounds,
it can not only grow biological material in a tiny area, but also
achieve sensitive layer functions directly, that is to replace the
traditional double-layer film or multilayer film. Moreover, the
membrane prepared in this study has the performance of
adjustable thickness, controllable function, growing on the
right location and shape.
