The effect of MWCNTs on the performance of α-sexithiophene
OTFT device and its gas-sensing property
TAI HuiLing*
, ZHANG Bo, DUAN ChengLi, XIE GuangZhong & JIANG YaDong
State Key Laboratory of Electronic Thin Films and Integrated Devices, School of Optoelectronic Information, University of Electronic Science
and Technology of China, Chengdu 610054, China
Received November 21, 2013; accepted April 2, 2014; published online May 13, 2014
In this paper, bottom contact organic thin-film transistor (OTFT) gas sensors were prepared. Silicon dioxide (SiO2) and titanium/aurum (Ti/Au) were used as the insulating layer and the electrode for the device, respectively. The multi-walled carbon
nanotubes (MWCNTs)/α-sexithiophene (α-6T) bilayer films were used as the active layer, and α-6T single layer sensitive film
was also prepared for comparison purpose. The electrical and trace NO2-sening properties of these two OTFT gas sensors were
tested and analyzed. The results showed that, the OTFT device based on MWCNTs/α-6T bilayer had obviously better electrical
properties, better stabilities and higher NO2-sening response values than the device with α-6T single layer, in which both the
carrier mobility (μ) and on/off current ratio enhanced two order of magnitude. The improved performance of bilayer OTFT can
be explained that MWCNTs acted as highly conducting bridges connecting the crystalline terraces in the α-6T film. Threshold
voltage (VT), carrier mobility, on/off current ratio and grid current which showed extremely similar variation trend as
source-drain current, were optional parameters to reveal the gas-sensing characteristic of OTFT gas sensors. Morphology
analysis showed that the special feature of MWCNTs had certain influence on the gas-sensing properties.

1 Introduction
In recent years, organic thin-film transistors (OTFTs) have
received extensive attention as basic electronic elements,
used for items such as display backplane, radio frequency
identification tags, and smart cards et al. Variety of inexpensive, widely used consumer goods [1–3] are the potential application of OTFTs. Laurs and Heiland [4] first proposed to take the OTFT as a gas sensor in the study of electrical and optical properties of phthalocyanine films. Much
research efforts to use OTFT as chemical sensing platforms
for vapor and liquid phase detection of explosives, toxins
and biochemicals have been devoted in pursuit of good levels of selectivity, reliability, and reproducibility at low cost
[5,6]. Vapor sensing in OTFTs relies on the charge transferring in the channel region. The diffusion of gaseous molecules through the grain boundaries of the organic active
layer into the channel region is critical. Therefore, the sensitive materials and film process are the core of the OTFT
sensors. Until now, organic semiconductor materials such as
phthalocyanines, oligothiophenes, pentacene, α-sexithiophene (α-6T) etc. [7–9], are mainly used for the active layer,
in which α-6T single layer film OTFTs has been studied to
detect trace NO2 and volatile organic compounds [10,11].
Despite ongoing advancements in material functionality
and processing technologies, OTFTs are somewhat limited
in terms of mobility and lifetime of devices, which hinder
1102 Tai H L, et al. Sci China Tech Sci June (2014) Vol.57 No.6
their application on a wider scale [12,13]. A number of
methods have been developed to improve the electrical
conductivity and practical use of OTFTs, such as doping
carbon nanotubes (CNTs) or metal oxide materials, etc
[13,14]. CNTs have attracted a lot of attention in many areas, because of their unique structure and electrical properties [15]. In the field of gas sensors, the sensing mechanisms
of NO2 and O2 gases have been reported when CNTs are
used as sensitive layers of chemical sensors [16,17]. However, for all we know, the OTFTs based on CNTs and small
organic molecule bilayer films and their gas-sensing properties have not been reported.
In this manuscript, OTFT sensors based on multi-walled
carbon nanotubes (MWCNTs)/α-6T bilayer films and α-6T
single layer film were fabricated, respectively. The surface
morphologies of MWCNTs single layer, α-6T single layer
and MWCNTs/α-6T bilayer were characterized by scanning
electron microscope (SEM). The electrical properties, stabilities and NO2-sensing properties of sensors were tested
and compared. Threshold voltage, carrier mobility, on/off
current ratio and grid current of OTFT sensors were taken
as the parameters to reveal the gas sensing results.
2 Experiment
Typically, OTFT devices have two structures: top contact
and bottom contact device configuration, where source/
drain electrodes are located above or below the organic
semiconductor layer, respectively [18]. In this paper, bottom
contact OTFT devices with MWCNTs/α-6T bilayer and
α-6T single layer were adopted as shown in Figures 1(a)
and (b), respectively, as they could provide a more efficient
contact for analysis. Highly doped n-type monocrystalline
silicon was used as substrate with the thickness of 450 μm,
then 195 nm SiO2 was thermally oxidized as insulating layer on the substrate. The 20 nm titanium and 50 nm aurum
thin films were orderly prepared as the source/drain electrodes with the ratio of channel width to length as 640.
MWCNTs alcohol dispersant (purchased from Chengdu
Organic Chemicals Co. Ltd, Chinese Academy of Sciences,
Wt=2.0%) was diluted with alcohol as the concentration of
1.8 mg/mL, and ultrasonicated (KQ-250 digital controlled
ultrasonic machine, KunShan Province ultrasonic instrument factory) for 20 min. In view of the good electrical
conductivity of MWCNTs, depositing too much of
MWCNTs would influence the field effect characteristics of
OTFT devices, so the amount of MWCNTs should be controlled strictly. In our experiment, three different groups of
sensitive films, single α-6T film, single MWCNTs film and
MWCNTs/α-6T bilayer film, were developed, in which the
single α-6T and MWCNTs films were prepared for the
comparison purpose. First, 0.02, 0.04 and 0.06 mL
MWCNTs solution were spray-deposited [19,20] onto SiO2
layer using an airbrush (HOLDER180, Taiwan HD corp.)

3.2.1 The surface morphology characterizations of films
Sensitive films were the core of film-typed gas sensors. The
surface morphology of films had direct influence on electrical properties and gas-sensing characteristics of OTFT
sensors. The surface morphologies of MWCNTs single layer film, α-6T single layer film and MWCNTs/α-6T bilayer
films were observed by SEM with the magnification of
80000, as shown in Figures 4(a), (b) and (c), respectively.
From Figure 4(a), it was obviously seen that MWCNTs
are strewn and stratified discontinuously at random because
of its special shape and structure, which provided more
contact sites. And α-6T single layer film (Figure 4(b))
showed smooth surface, uniform grains and smaller grain
clearance, which played an important role to avoid hindering the lateral transport of injected carriers, resulting in excellent characteristics of α-6T. When α-6T molecules were
deposited on the MWCNTs film, the molecules reunion was
only just around the branches of MWCNTs as Figure 4(c).
It was postulated that some MWCNTs would be immersed
in α-6T molecules fully if the height of the tubes was lower
than the α-6T film, and others would surrounded with α-6T
molecules, as shown in Figure 4(c), indicating more adsorption sites than the single α-6T film.

voltage [22]. The comparison of response values between

3.2.4 Comparison of stability between bilayer and single
layer OTFT
Stability is one of important elements to estimate the practical properties of gas sensors. Figures 7 (a) and (b) show
the electrical stability researches of MWCNTs/α-6T bilayer
and α-6T single layer OTFT sensors in 30 days, respectively.
The testing interval was 10 days.
From Figures 7(a) and (b), it is obvious that the values of
IDS were declining when the time went on for the first 20
days, while the values kept unchanged after 20 days. The
phenomenon illustrates that the sensitive films of OTFTs
are largely influenced by some factors in atmosphere, such
as O2, water vapour, dust or others, and the influence
reached saturate condition in 20 days. However, MWCNTs/
α-6T bilayer OTFT whose current value became one fifth of
the initial value showed relatively better stability, compared
to the single layer one whose current value had become
1/250 of initial value in 30 days. The main reason was ascribed to both the good stability of MWCNTs and the coverage of α-6T. MWCNTs mainly decided the conductivity
of the bilayer films, which themselves performed good stability, and most of MWCNTs were located under the α-6T
layer and avoided some influence of external environment,
so the good conductivity was kept. The α-6T single layer
OTFT suffered severe impact from its pure active layer
which was completely exposed to environment. Figure 7(c)
shows the response values of MWCNTs/α-6T bilayer device
when exposed to 1 ppm NO2 within 30 days, while the single layer one was ignored because of its worse electrical
properties. Overall, the bilayer device had extremely similar
response values in 30 days. Although the current of device
was influenced by other factors, response value was almost
kept as consistent as the initial value. From the results, it
was found that the MWCNTs/α-6T bilayer OTFT showed
better stability and had a longer lifetime.

3.3 The NO2-sensing properties of MWCNTs/α-6T
bilayer OTFTs
3.3.1 Trace NO2 sensing properties based on MWCNTs/
α-6T OTFT
The trace NO2-sensing properties of MWCNTs/α-6T bilayer
OTFT sensors were further measured and discussed. Figure
8 displayed the real-time response curve of the bilayer
OTFT sensor exposed to 0.2–1 ppm NO2 gas atmosphere
with dry air when the carrier gas at room temperature. The
working point (VDS=40 V, VGS=40 V) had been chosen in
the saturated region of the I-V characteristics.
It could be clearly seen that MWCNTs/α-6T bilayer
OTFT sensor behaved in its response to trace NO2 ranging
from 0.2 to 1 ppm at room temperature. IDS increased dramatically when the device was exposed to NO2, which
might be ascribed to the adsorption effect between NO2 gas
molecules and the polar-bonding of MWCNTs/α-6T bilayer
films. And the effect was reversible. Also, the excellent
reproducibility and reversibility were indicated. Therefore,
it is very promising to take the MWCNTs/α-6T bilayer
OTFT as gas sensors to detect trace NO2.
The relationship of response values vs. NO2 concentration was fitted using the quadratic polynomial fitting, and
the curve was included in Figure 8. As shown in Figure 8, it
can be supposed that the absorption of NO2 gas molecules
on the surface of sensitive films can be explained by the
Langmiur adsorption theory to a certain extent. That was,
the IDS varied in a non-linear way with NO2 gas concentration. The interaction between the gas molecules and the
surface of the active layer is an extremely complex phenomenon, and the structure and morphology of the thin film
are the determinant parameters of this interaction [23].
Since α-6T in the MWCNTs/α-6T bilayer films were polycrystalline with uniform grains based on the results of SEM
images, it is reasonable to believe that NO2 may diffuse
along uniform grain boundaries, but not appreciably within
grains. Therefore the interaction is limited to the surface and
near-surface region [24].

3.3.3 Selectivity of MWCNTs/α-6T bilayer OTFT sensor
The selectivity of MWCNTs/α-6T bilayer OTFT sensor was
studied through detecting other air pollution gases, such as
O3, CO, CO2, H2S, NH3, SO2, and formaldehyde (CH2O).
The results are shown in Figure 11. The sensor had no response to other trace gases, and 50 ppm was chose as the
concentration of analytes. The response values of any other
analytes, especially for inert gases, were much lower than
the value to 1 ppm NO2 which had special reaction to bilayer films. In experiment, it was found that the device
showed poor recovery property when exposed to most of
the analytes, except for NO2. Therefore, the MWCNTs/α-6T
bilayer OTFT sensor was promising to detect NO2 in the
complicated gas mixture in low concentration.
