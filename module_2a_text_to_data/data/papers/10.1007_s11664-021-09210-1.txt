The exceptional properties of gallium nitride (GaN) such as distinctive band gap and strong chemical stability make it promising as a semiconductor material to detect and select ions, liquids and gases. Sensing characteristics of an extended gate field effect transistor (EGFET)-based undoped N-type GaN as a pH sensor was investigated by using six buffer solutions with pH range 2–12 at room temperature. Distinctive current-voltage (I–V) characteristics of EGFET with gate controllability were observed in a solution. The surface potential of the membrane at the sensing area is controlled by Ag/AgCl reference electrode via aqueous solution. The sensitivity in terms of reference voltage in the linear region was 24.72 mV/pH with a good linearity. There is another function in the saturation region in the expression via drain-source current (IDS), and the outputs indicated that to 0.39 (μA)1/2/pH.IDSsensitivity proved to be a reliable outcome as a linear response along the pH range. As an indicator of the stability and the reversibility of our sensor, we measured the hysteresis with two loops. The results showed a low value of hysteresis depth of 14.98 mV and 13.06 mV for the acidic side, 30.91 and 32.72 for the basic side.

Gallium nitride (GaN) is a semiconductor with a binary III/V direct band gap of 3.4 eV. GaN is considered a hard material with superior chemical stability and is non-toxic. These features make it an appropriate material for: high-power, high-frequency and high-temperature optoelectronic devices, solar cells, laser diodes, nanoscale electronics devices, transistors, gas sensors, and chemical and biochemical sensors.1,2,3The monitoring of biological substances, heavy metals, moderation, and temperature by some sensors have a vital development trend, which enhances the protection of the environment and human health.4,5,6,7Nanomaterial-based sensors for environmental monitoring exhibited excellent potential in detection of trace contaminants due to the unique features of nanomaterials such as strong adsorption capacity, high surface area and reactivity, and large catalytic efficiency.8,9

GaN has been used previously for pH sensing, because it has high electron mobility, large sheet carrier concentration and fast response to ion detection. Also, at high temperatures, it operates with low noise because of its superior resistance to environmental effects and large energy band gap.10Restrictively, the pH value influences many chemical and biological reactions. pH sensors are used extensively to ensure human health and monitor chemical and biological reactions, water and food quality, and production and life processes.11,12,13

Many factors can affect the pH sensitivity of GaN such as doping, sheet carrier concentration, and temperature. The pH sensitivity increased form 52.3 mV/pH to 69.5 mV/pH when the temperature increased from 20°C to 80°C, respectively.

Recently, a new method has been used to measure pH sensitivity by the extended gate field-effect transistor (EGFET). In this case, the commercial MOSFET is kept away from the chemical and biological environment. In this work, we investigate undoped N-type GaN/Al2O3as a pH sensing membrane by using the EGFET. The pH sensitivity in two terms (voltage and current) was extracted. Current sensitivity was not extracted in previous studies that used GaN based EGFET. In the case of N-type gallium nitride, the electrons constitute the majority carriers, which are most attractive for cations in the electrolyte that align on the surface of the semiconductors.30The characterization of sensitivity and stability of GaN along pH range 2–12 will be characterized.

In order to explain the low pH sensitivity, the hydrogen cation or hydroxide anion interaction can govern the pH sensitivity during which either the cation or the anion in the electrolyte changes, whereas the other stays the same. Based on the previous studies, the GaN surface is more selective for anions than cations. Furthermore, the partial oxidization of the GaN surface increases the hydroxide sites on the outer gallium surface. Based on that, the response to pH results in the selective interaction of the GaN surface with OH-. With more surface oxidation, the OH- interaction with the negative Ga surface and the H+interaction with the generated hydroxide sites generate a mixed potential.34,35The other decisive factor in decreasing the pH sensitivity is the smoothness of the sensing surface, as we note that the surface roughness of GaN is low, and thus there are fewer surface sites, and this is shown by Eqs.2and3. The amount of sensing sites can be decreased by decreasing the surface area (Eq.4), which decreases the electrode–electrolyte interface leading to fewer surface sites per unit area and EDL, thus directly reducing the pH sensitivity.36In comparison with previous studies that used GaN as a pH sensor, shown in TableI, the variation in pH sensitivity values is affected by many factors, including the sensor's platform and the substrate on which GaN is grown, in addition to the different electrical and structural properties of GaN from one study to another. It is noted that the pH sensitivity of current study is lower than the values extracted in previous studies, due to the use of undoped wafer, which increases its resistivity and thus affects the pH sensitivity value. Also, the pH sensitivity value is affected by the measured pH range, which becomes higher value with lower range.

The hysteresis is called the memory effect; it is influenced by the slow response of the pH-EGFET. Hysteresis is produced via a slow response, and the depth of the hysteresis loop represents a function of the time constant of the slow response and loop time.37The characteristics of hysteresis were extracted from theV–Tplot for two loops of the buffer solutions with pH values (7–4–7–10–7) and (7–2–7–12–7). The sensing membrane was immersed in each pH buffer solution for 5 min.The hysteresis was subsequently obtained by taking the difference between the final value and the initial value of pH 7.38From Fig.6, the hysteresis depth is smaller on the acid side (14.98  mV and 13.06 mV) than that of on the basic side (30.91 mV and 32.72 mV) because the different size of H+and OH−ions leads to slower diffusion of OH−ions than the diffusion of H+ions.39

In this article, we presented the GaN/Al2O3-based EGFET pH sensor. The study concluded a positive and negative linear correlation with superior linearity 0.98 between the reference voltage (VREF) and the drain–source current (IDS), respectively, versus pH value. The voltage sensitivity was 24.72 mV/pH, while the current sensitivity obtained 0.39 (µA)1/2/pH. The main important factor as an indicator for stability and reversibility to field effect transistors in applications of ion detecting is the hysteresis phenomenon. The hysteresis depths at pH 7 in cycle 7–4–7–10–7 were 14.98 mV and 30.91 mV for acidic and basic sides, respectively. The results showed stable detecting executions which qualify the GaN/Al2O3-based EGFET pH sensor to be used for commercial devices. In addition, size controlling of sensing area, long sensing of pH range, good repetition accuracy, and the features of GaN such as not toxic material give this sensor a good level of practicability.

Fig.2b. shows a linear negative correlation between square root ofIDSversus pH value.In order to explain the low pH sensitivity, the hydrogen cation or hydroxide anion interaction can govern the pH sensitivity during which either the cation or the anion in the electrolyte changes, whereas the other stays the same. Based on the previous studies, the GaN surface is more selective for anions than cations. Furthermore, the partial oxidization of the GaN surface increases the hydroxide sites on the outer gallium surface. Based on that, the response to pH results in the selective interaction of the GaN surface with OH-. With more surface oxidation, the OH- interaction with the negative Ga surface and the H+interaction with the generated hydroxide sites generate a mixed potential.34,35The other decisive factor in decreasing the pH sensitivity is the smoothness of the sensing surface, as we note that the surface roughness of GaN is low, and thus there are fewer surface sites, and this is shown by Eqs.2and3. The amount of sensing sites can be decreased by decreasing the surface area (Eq.4), which decreases the electrode–electrolyte interface leading to fewer surface sites per unit area and EDL, thus directly reducing the pH sensitivity.36In comparison with previous studies that used GaN as a pH sensor, shown in TableI, the variation in pH sensitivity values is affected by many factors, including the sensor's platform and the substrate on which GaN is grown, in addition to the different electrical and structural properties of GaN from one study to another. It is noted that the pH sensitivity of current study is lower than the values extracted in previous studies, due to the use of undoped wafer, which increases its resistivity and thus affects the pH sensitivity value. Also, the pH sensitivity value is affected by the measured pH range, which becomes higher value with lower range.The hysteresis is called the memory effect; it is influenced by the slow response of the pH-EGFET. The study concluded a positive and negative linear correlation with superior linearity 0.98 between the reference voltage (VREF) and the drain–source current (IDS), respectively, versus pH value. The voltage sensitivity was 24.72 mV/pH, while the current sensitivity obtained 0.39 (µA)1/2/pH. The main important factor as an indicator for stability and reversibility to field effect transistors in applications of ion detecting is the hysteresis phenomenon. The hysteresis depths at pH 7 in cycle 7–4–7–10–7 were 14.98 mV and 30.91 mV for acidic and basic sides, respectively. The results showed stable detecting executions which qualify the GaN/Al2O3-based EGFET pH sensor to be used for commercial devices.
