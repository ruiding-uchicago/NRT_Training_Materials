Recently, solvothermal technique was used to explore and construct 3D(micro/nano)V2O5growth on (M-PSi) as a sensing gate for the pH EG-FET device. The sensor has high sensitivity and a linear response in the pH range of 2–12, as well as relatively high hysteresis. However, around pH 12, the membrane gradually degrades and redox species in solution cause significant pH measurement errors. To overcome the limitations of a manufactured sensor, a thick layer of selective proton permeable Nafion was coated as a protective layer over the sensing gate 3D(micro/nano)V2O5using the drop-casting method. Utilizing an x-ray diffractometer and field emission scanning electron microscopy, the structural and morphological features of the sensing layer were analyzed. The Nafion coating on the sensing gate pH EG-FET device decreased its sensitivity, modified linearity in the linear and saturation regions, and reduced hysteresis with significantly less drift. The sensor's great stability is due to Nafion's chemical resistance and capability to transport protons, which lowers the interference produced by anionic oxidizing or redwing (redox) species on the response of an uncoated electrode. Nafion can be considered a potential candidate as a protective layer for different MOx based pH sensors in a complicated environment for various biomedical applications.

The invention of electrochemical pH devices (conductimetric, potentiometric, resistive, and capacitive) has received substantial attention during the last few decades [1,2,3,4]. Extended gate-field effect transistor (EG-FET) is a form of potentiometric pH-sensing device that is a highly promising method for detecting pH, glucose, salt, as well as blood, urine, and other biological fluids [5]. An industrial Metal Oxide Semiconductor-Field Effect Transistor (MOS-FET) is separated from the chemical ambient, and a biochemical sensor membrane (extended gate) is attached to the gate of the MOS-FET. The thin film utilized as the expanded gate of the FET and submerged in the electrolyte solution with a working reference electrode will generate a potential that is affected by the pH of the solution [6,7]. Numerous materials and configurations have been researched for the creation of sensitive electrodes for these sensors. Metal Oxide (MOx)-based pH EG-FET sensors have gained considerable interest owing to its high sensitivity, short reaction time, simplicity of downsizing, and biocompatibility of the majority of MOx. Several materials, notably semiconductor metal oxides including ZnO [8,9], TiO2[10], PdO [11], and IrO2[12], were evaluated as sensing membranes. Unfortunately, as the majority of metal oxides are electronic conductors, they also react with redox species present in solution, resulting in significant pH measurement errors. And certain metal oxides were easy degradation in high or low pH. However, display poor performance and require specific modifications to be employed as membranes for severe pH levels [1,13,14]. However, that it is feasible to remove or dampen many of these interferences by covering the metal oxide electrode with Nafion [14,15]. Nafion is a randomized copolymer made up of a hydrophobic fluorocarbon chain and hydrophilic sulfone groups at its ends. It is a member of the group of perfluorinated sulfonic acid ionomers recognized for their physical and chemical stability, ionic conductivity, and use as proton exchange membranes [14,16]. Due to its high proton conductivity compared to that of other cations, Nafion layer functions as a selectively permeable membrane for H+ions. Consequently, it is employed as a proton selective membrane in fuel cells and pH sensors [17,18]. Drop-casting method is commonly used to coat solid state electrode with a semipermeable Nafion membrane layer for protection [14,15]. The process of drop_casting involves spreading nanoparticle dispersion onto a substrate and letting it to dry at regulated pressure and temperature. Theoretically, the thick of a film is dependent on the volume of dispersion employed and the particle concentration, which are both easily variable [19]. In our previous study, we employed the solvothermal approach to manufacture a sensing membrane of 3D(micro/nano)V2O5spheres on Macro-Porous Silicon (M-PSi) and used it as an extended gate for a pH EG-FET sensor because of the possibility of degradation caused by long-term operation under various chemical conditions [20]. In this work, a 3D(micro/nano)V2O5/M-PSi pH EG-FET sensor was upgraded with a Nafion protective barrier via simple pipette drop casting of Nafion dispersion over the sensing membrane (extended gate) to improve sensor stability.

In our prior research, 3D(micro/nano)V2O5spheres were produced on M-PSi using the solvothermal technique; more information about sample synthesis can be found in reference [20]. The substrate (M-PSi) was manufactured by electrochemically etching a wafer ofp-type monocrystalline Si-wafer. The electrolyte was produced by mixing HF acid and ethanol in a volume ratio of 1:4. Throughout the 60-min etching procedure, the current was maintained at 10 mA/cm2.

On M-PSi, 3D(micro/nano)V2O5spheres were then produced using the solvothermal approach. 0.01 M of NH4VO3was dissolved in 100 mL of ethanol. A few drops of HNO3were added to the solvents to adjust the solution's pH (3–4). 80 mL of the liquid is placed into a 100 mL stainless steel autoclave coated with Teflon that contains the substrate M-PSi. The M-PSi was angled toward the Teflon lined wall and the solution-facing porous layer. The closed autoclave then was preheated for 12 h at 180 °C in an electrical furnace before being progressively cooled to ambient temperature. Subsequently, the resulting sample was calcined in a quartz heater at 550 °C for 1 h under moist oxygen at a flow rate of 4 mL/min.

Using the drop-casting method, the 3D(micro/nano)V2O5/M-PSi electrodes were upgraded with a Nafion protective barrier. A substantial amount of Nafion perfluorinated resin solution (conc. 5% wt. in water and aliphatic alcohols (15–20%), SGMA-ALDICH Co., USA) is deposited on the sensing gate to prevent any surface fracture for the leaking solution, to protect V2O5, and to improve its proton conductivity. A 10 µL dosage of dispersion with Nafion, drip it from a micropipe over a 1.5 cm2patch of V2O5membrane and allow it to dry overnight. The structure of the untreated membranes and Nafion-coated 3D(micro/nano)V2O5/M-PSi was examined using an X-ray diffraction system (XRD) (Burker D8-advance). The XRD spectra were obtained using Cu-Kα (λ= 1.54178 Å) radiation, which was generated at 40 kV and 40 mA, 2θscanning between 20 and 80° with a step size of 0.02°. AX′ pert high-score plus computer software with search and match capabilities was then used to identify the various phases present in the synthesized samples. An analytical FE-SEM was utilized to evaluate the topography and layer thickness of the produced membrane (Nova Nano SEM-450) which was operating at an accelerating voltage of 10 kV and a working distance of 4.9 mm. This apparatus, which was combined with energy dispersive X-ray spectroscopy (EDXS), could also evaluate the chemical composition of the sensor membrane.

The pH sensor system setup is shown in Fig.2, which comprises of two configurations: Fig.2a evaluates the membrane's pH sensitivity and linearity. A pH EG-FET sensor was easily constructed by connecting the sensing membrane (extended gate) to a commercial N-type MOS-FET (CD4007UB, Texas Instruments, USA). The sensor membrane is affixed inside a chamber filled with buffer solution. Using two Keithley-2400 source measurement units (SMUs), the (I-V) curves were determined. A GPIB-USB connection was used to link the devices to a personal computer (PC). One SMU-2400 was connected to the source and drain terminals of the commercial MOS-FET to provide the drain-source voltage (VDS) and measure the drain-source output current (IDS), while the other SMU-2400 was used to apply the reference voltage (Vref) to the standard reference electrode Ag/AgCl, which was immersed in the buffer solution in order to give a constant voltage to the sensing membrane. The second apparatus seen in Fig.2b was employed to evaluate the sensor's hysteresis and drift rate. In the absence of MOS-FET, one of the SMU-2400 was modified to measure the output voltage as a function of time with a direct connection between the reference electrode and extended gate.

X-ray diffraction confirmed the phase purity of bare and Nafion-coated 3D(micro/nano)V2O5/M-PSi membranes, which are depicted in Fig.3. For the bare 3D(micro/nano)V2O5/M-PSi, the diffraction peaks in XRD spectrum confirm the existence of the V2O5on the M-PSi. The intense peaks evident at 2θ= 15.636°, 20.488°, 21.930°, 26.385°, 31.028°, 32.594°, 34.541°, and 51.408° denote (200), (001), (101), (110), (031), (011), (310), and (212) planes, respectively. These peaks correspond to those of the conventional orthorhombic V2O5phase (ICSD (V2O5)-: 01-077-2418, Pmmn), while the strong peak at (69.362°) returns back to the substrate M-PSi (100). In comparison, no obvious peaks were identified in the XRD spectra of membranes coated with Nafion. This is due to the membrane's thick coating of amorphous (non-crystalline) Nafion polymer. The XRD spectrum exhibits a hump at roughly 2θ= 16.5°, confirming the amorphous nature of membrane. This accorded with those published in the literature [21,22,23,24]. The amorphous hump at 16.5° was associated with the crystal scattering of the polyfluorocarbon-chains in the Nafion layer, which overlapped the X-ray dispersion from the amorphous part of the Nafion at smaller Bragg angles [23,24].

The Energy Dispersive X-Ray validated the chemical components of untreated and Nafion-coated 3D(micro/nano)V2O5/M-PSi membranes. The EDX spectrum of a 3D(micro/nano)V2O5/M-PSi membrane covered with Nafion is shown in Fig.5. The inset table in Fig.5's detected components on the uncoated and Nafion-coated membranes is provided. The EDX analysis suggests the presence of V and O elements with a V to O atomic ratio of 0.397, which is near to the stoichiometric ratio of V2O5, in addition to the Si from the underlying M-PSi substrate. On the other hand, EDX was applied to two separate regions (points) of the Nafion-coated membrane: the surface (point_1) and inside the pore (point_2). The discovery indicates the presence of fluorine, carbon, and trace amounts of vanadium on the surface. Moreover, the presence of a significant quantity of Nafion and carbon inside the pore insure that Nafion was absorbed by the pore. Both FE-SEM and EDX demonstrated that no cape exists between Nafion and V2O5inside the pore. This facilitates H+transport through Nafion to the V2O5that grows on the wall of M-PSi.

In the last year, the 3D(micro/nano)V2O5sphere growth on (M-PSi) was studied and manufactured in our laboratory using solvothermal method as a sensing gate for the ph-EGEFT device. In our investigation, the sensing gate of 3D(micro/nano)V2O5/M-PSi demonstrates very high sensitivity and a linear response in acid and base buffer solutions, as well as reasonably poor hysteresis [20]. As the sample is generated in an acidic environment, the membrane progressively degrades at pH 12. This deterioration is obvious due to the solution being converted to a suspension solution this happens after a single measurement cycle. V2O5degradation was documented by Eder J. Guidelli at pH levels greater than 10 [25]. The system's behavior over long-term operation or after several reuses of the sensing membrane in buffer solution is unpredictable in many instances; deterioration might occur during measurements; therefore, the device's response may not provide accurate data. Thus, a thick layer of Nafion was dispersed across the sensing membrane using the drop-casting technique. However, the published reference [20] clarifies the pH-sensing mechanism of adsorbed surface ions in pH-EGFET. The pH sensitivity and linearity of the sensor were derived from the (I-V) curves by graphing the linear relation between (Vrefvs. pH) at anIDSvalue of 0.4 mA and ((\(\sqrt{{I}_{DS}}\)) vs. pH) at aVDSvalue of 3 V for the linear and saturation regions, as shown in Fig.7.

The 3D(micro/nano)V2O5/M-PSi pH EG-FET device generated in a previous work exhibited a higher pH sensitivity and a moderate linearity of (91.1 mV/pH, and 99.67% for the linear region, and 2.57, and 97.74% for the saturation region, respectively) [20]. This is a result of the high surface to volume ratio and oxide state of the V2O5nonmetric structure growth on the surface and inside M-PSi without the entire closing of the pores, which facilitates the penetration of the buffer solution within the M-PSi, resulting in a higher effective sensing area. The pH sensitivity and linearity values for Nafion-coated 3D(micro/nano)V2O5/M-PSi are 74.8 mV/pH and 99.98% for the linear region, and 2.02 μA/pH, and 99.95% for the saturation region, respectively. According to the findings, the coated membrane displayed comparatively reduced sensitivity and enhanced linearity values. As is known, the EG-FET exterior component (sensing gate) of a low impedance substance is employed to expose the device directly to the buffer solution and communicate the potential difference to the gate of a commercial MOS-FET [5,6,7]. The presence of Nafion inside the pore and on the top of the sensing membrane will reduce the membrane's electric conductivity, resulting in a reduced signal transmission to the standard MOSFET. In contrast to pH IS_FET sensors, where a high impedance material is employed as a sensing gate, several studies demonstrate an improvement in sensitivity using a Nafion-coated sensing gate [6,26]. In addition, when the pore is entirely filled with Nafion, the pH sensitivity may be changed due to the reduced direct contact between the electrolyzed and sensing gate interface. However, the surface layer of Nafion is cation permselective, minimizing or attenuating various anionic interferences and maintaining steady Cl- activities on the sensor surface therefore, a stable electrode voltage increased linearity of pH EG-FET [1].

Generally, hysteresis is connected with the chemical reactions between both the ions present in the buffer solution as well as the slow reacting surface sites beneath the surface of the sensing layer, also known as buried sites and/or imperfections, which is referred to as the pH_sensor's delay [7,27]. The membranes' hysteresis was measured using the apparatus described in Fig.2b. This setup was used to evaluate the voltage difference between both the working electrode and the sensing membrane as a time-varying parameter for an assortment of buffer solutions with random pH loops of (pH7-pH4-pH7-pH10-pH7) with each loop spanning 5 min. As shown in Fig.8(left side), the (Vout-Time) graph was then displayed, and the hysteresis was derived by computing the responses of double measures in buffer solution of pH7 throughout cycle measurements. The hysteresis values are 11 mV for uncoated 3D(micro/nano)V2O5/M-PSi and 5.3 mV for Nafion-coated 3D(micro/nano)V2O5/M-PSi. The high value of hysteresis for uncoated devices relative to Nafion-coated devices is caused by the huge porosity of the sensing gate, which is caused by the absorption of many ions (species) into the pores, making it difficult to remove them after each test.

Hysteresis and Drift rate forauncoated andbNafion-coated 3D(micro/nano) V2O5/M-PSi in pH buffer solutions (10, 7 and 4)

Drift is an inherent issue with bio-FETs, especially EG-FET, since it causes device instability and a decline in measurement precision with time. It is a gradual, steady, temporal variation in the threshold voltage's value [28]. In 60 min, the drift behavior of uncoated and Nafion-coated 3D(micro/nano)V2O5/M-PSi was investigated in several buffer solutions (pH10, pH7, and pH4). Figure8(right side) displays the drift rate of the uncoated and Nafion-coated 3D(micro/nano) V2O5/M-PSi, which was 24.6 µV/s (pH10), 8.2 µV/s (pH7) and 10.6 µV/sec (pH4) for the uncoated and dropped to 0.16 µV/s (pH7), 0.008 µV/sec (pH7) and 0.13 µV/sec for (pH10) for the Nafion-coated membrane. The present Nafion will prevent change in the oxidation number of vanadium or deterioration of the sensing membrane, which may lead to variable ion sensing qualities and erroneous pH-sensing responses as a result of the films' exposure to many ion concentrations during measurements.

Even while overlaying the sensing gate of a pH EG-FETs device with a thin membrane of Nafion diminished its sensitivity, it modified the linearity in the linear and saturation areas and decreased hysteresis with even less drift. Additionally, one of the disadvantages of a Metal Oxide (MOx)-based pH sensors, linked to its sensitivity to redox species in the solution, which results to inaccurate pH measurements, may be eliminated. In contrast, due to the interference of lipids and proteins, the use of metal oxide pH-electrodes, which may adsorb on the sensor surface and impede measurements, is prohibited. This is particularly significant for the capacity to monitor pH in complex matrices, such as blood or food samples. This limitation may be avoided by covering the pH sensitive section with a semipermeable layer which allows only hydrogen ions to pass, so preserving the sensor surface from contaminating [14,15]. Since Nafion is resistant to both alkaline and acidic solutions, the device will last longer and the pH EG-FET sensor's measuring range will change or extend.

In biosensors, the response of samples as a function of time is critical. Thus, a protective layer of Nafion selective proton permeable was deposited over the sensing gate of 3D(micro/nano)V2O5by drop casting of 10 µL of the 5% by weight Nafion solution, with the aim of boosting the long-term stability of the 3D(micro/nano)V2O5EG-FET pH-sensor. The findings demonstrated that XRD revealed the amorphous nature of the synthesized Nafion-coated membrane, while FE-SEM and EDX suggested the creation of a thick, smooth, and crack-free layer of Nafion that had penetrated into the pores. The Nafion-coated sensor's sensitivity decreased to 74.8 mV/pH, hysteresis decreased to 5.3 mV, and the sensor drift (0.16 µV/sec, 0.008 µV/sec and 0.13 µV/sec for pH10, pH7, and pH4, respectively). Compared to the coated sensor, the uncoated sensor had a sensitivity of 91.1 mV/pH, a hysteresis of 11 mV, and drift values of (24.6 µV/sec, 8.2 µV/sec, and 10.6 µV/sec for pH10, pH7, and pH4, respectively. The presence of Nafion prevents solution from seeping into pores and degrading V2O5, thereby preventing contamination of the electrode.

AbstractRecently, solvothermal technique was used to explore and construct 3D(micro/nano)V2O5growth on (M-PSi) as a sensing gate for the pH EG-FET device. The sensor has high sensitivity and a linear response in the pH range of 2–12, as well as relatively high hysteresis. However, around pH 12, the membrane gradually degrades and redox species in solution cause significant pH measurement errors. To overcome the limitations of a manufactured sensor, a thick layer of selective proton permeable Nafion was coated as a protective layer over the sensing gate 3D(micro/nano)V2O5using the drop-casting method. Utilizing an x-ray diffractometer and field emission scanning electron microscopy, the structural and morphological features of the sensing layer were analyzed. The Nafion coating on the sensing gate pH EG-FET device decreased its sensitivity, modified linearity in the linear and saturation regions, and reduced hysteresis with significantly less drift. The sensor's great stability is due to Nafion's chemical resistance and capability to transport protons, which lowers the interference produced by anionic oxidizing or redwing (redox) species on the response of an uncoated electrode. Extended gate-field effect transistor (EG-FET) is a form of potentiometric pH-sensing device that is a highly promising method for detecting pH, glucose, salt, as well as blood, urine, and other biological fluids [5]. An industrial Metal Oxide Semiconductor-Field Effect Transistor (MOS-FET) is separated from the chemical ambient, and a biochemical sensor membrane (extended gate) is attached to the gate of the MOS-FET. The thin film utilized as the expanded gate of the FET and submerged in the electrolyte solution with a working reference electrode will generate a potential that is affected by the pH of the solution [6,7]. Numerous materials and configurations have been researched for the creation of sensitive electrodes for these sensors. Metal Oxide (MOx)-based pH EG-FET sensors have gained considerable interest owing to its high sensitivity, short reaction time, simplicity of downsizing, and biocompatibility of the majority of MOx. Several materials, notably semiconductor metal oxides including ZnO [8,9], TiO2[10], PdO [11], and IrO2[12], were evaluated as sensing membranes. Unfortunately, as the majority of metal oxides are electronic conductors, they also react with redox species present in solution, resulting in significant pH measurement errors. And certain metal oxides were easy degradation in high or low pH. However, display poor performance and require specific modifications to be employed as membranes for severe pH levels [1,13,14]. However, that it is feasible to remove or dampen many of these interferences by covering the metal oxide electrode with Nafion [14,15]. Nafion is a randomized copolymer made up of a hydrophobic fluorocarbon chain and hydrophilic sulfone groups at its ends. It is a member of the group of perfluorinated sulfonic acid ionomers recognized for their physical and chemical stability, ionic conductivity, and use as proton exchange membranes [14,16]. Due to its high proton conductivity compared to that of other cations, Nafion layer functions as a selectively permeable membrane for H+ions. Consequently, it is employed as a proton selective membrane in fuel cells and pH sensors [17,18]. Drop-casting method is commonly used to coat solid state electrode with a semipermeable Nafion membrane layer for protection [14,15]. The process of drop_casting involves spreading nanoparticle dispersion onto a substrate and letting it to dry at regulated pressure and temperature. Theoretically, the thick of a film is dependent on the volume of dispersion employed and the particle concentration, which are both easily variable [19]. In our previous study, we employed the solvothermal approach to manufacture a sensing membrane of 3D(micro/nano)V2O5spheres on Macro-Porous Silicon (M-PSi) and used it as an extended gate for a pH EG-FET sensor because of the possibility of degradation caused by long-term operation under various chemical conditions [20]. In this work, a 3D(micro/nano)V2O5/M-PSi pH EG-FET sensor was upgraded with a Nafion protective barrier via simple pipette drop casting of Nafion dispersion over the sensing membrane (extended gate) to improve sensor stability.Experimental detailsMembrane synthesisIn our prior research, 3D(micro/nano)V2O5spheres were produced on M-PSi using the solvothermal technique; more information about sample synthesis can be found in reference [20]. The substrate (M-PSi) was manufactured by electrochemically etching a wafer ofp-type monocrystalline Si-wafer. The electrolyte was produced by mixing HF acid and ethanol in a volume ratio of 1:4. Throughout the 60-min etching procedure, the current was maintained at 10 mA/cm2.On M-PSi, 3D(micro/nano)V2O5spheres were then produced using the solvothermal approach. 0.01 M of NH4VO3was dissolved in 100 mL of ethanol. A few drops of HNO3were added to the solvents to adjust the solution's pH (3–4). 80 mL of the liquid is placed into a 100 mL stainless steel autoclave coated with Teflon that contains the substrate M-PSi. The M-PSi was angled toward the Teflon lined wall and the solution-facing porous layer. The closed autoclave then was preheated for 12 h at 180 °C in an electrical furnace before being progressively cooled to ambient temperature. Subsequently, the resulting sample was calcined in a quartz heater at 550 °C for 1 h under moist oxygen at a flow rate of 4 mL/min.Using the drop-casting method, the 3D(micro/nano)V2O5/M-PSi electrodes were upgraded with a Nafion protective barrier. A substantial amount of Nafion perfluorinated resin solution (conc. 5% wt. in water and aliphatic alcohols (15–20%), SGMA-ALDICH Co., USA) is deposited on the sensing gate to prevent any surface fracture for the leaking solution, to protect V2O5, and to improve its proton conductivity. A 10 µL dosage of dispersion with Nafion, drip it from a micropipe over a 1.5 cm2patch of V2O5membrane and allow it to dry overnight. According to the findings, the coated membrane displayed comparatively reduced sensitivity and enhanced linearity values. As is known, the EG-FET exterior component (sensing gate) of a low impedance substance is employed to expose the device directly to the buffer solution and communicate the potential difference to the gate of a commercial MOS-FET [5,6,7]. The presence of Nafion inside the pore and on the top of the sensing membrane will reduce the membrane's electric conductivity, resulting in a reduced signal transmission to the standard MOSFET. In contrast to pH IS_FET sensors, where a high impedance material is employed as a sensing gate, several studies demonstrate an improvement in sensitivity using a Nafion-coated sensing gate [6,26]. In addition, when the pore is entirely filled with Nafion, the pH sensitivity may be changed due to the reduced direct contact between the electrolyzed and sensing gate interface. However, the surface layer of Nafion is cation permselective, minimizing or attenuating various anionic interferences and maintaining steady Cl- activities on the sensor surface therefore, a stable electrode voltage increased linearity of pH EG-FET [1].Hysteresis and driftGenerally, hysteresis is connected with the chemical reactions between both the ions present in the buffer solution as well as the slow reacting surface sites beneath the surface of the sensing layer, also known as buried sites and/or imperfections, which is referred to as the pH_sensor's delay [7,27]. The membranes' hysteresis was measured using the apparatus described in Fig.2b. This setup was used to evaluate the voltage difference between both the working electrode and the sensing membrane as a time-varying parameter for an assortment of buffer solutions with random pH loops of (pH7-pH4-pH7-pH10-pH7) with each loop spanning 5 min. As shown in Fig.8(left side), the (Vout-Time) graph was then displayed, and the hysteresis was derived by computing the responses of double measures in buffer solution of pH7 throughout cycle measurements. The hysteresis values are 11 mV for uncoated 3D(micro/nano)V2O5/M-PSi and 5.3 mV for Nafion-coated 3D(micro/nano)V2O5/M-PSi. The high value of hysteresis for uncoated devices relative to Nafion-coated devices is caused by the huge porosity of the sensing gate, which is caused by the absorption of many ions (species) into the pores, making it difficult to remove them after each test.Figure 8Hysteresis and Drift rate forauncoated andbNafion-coated 3D(micro/nano) V2O5/M-PSi in pH buffer solutions (10, 7 and 4)Full size imageDrift is an inherent issue with bio-FETs, especially EG-FET, since it causes device instability and a decline in measurement precision with time. It is a gradual, steady, temporal variation in the threshold voltage's value [28]. In 60 min, the drift behavior of uncoated and Nafion-coated 3D(micro/nano)V2O5/M-PSi was investigated in several buffer solutions (pH10, pH7, and pH4). Figure8(right side) displays the drift rate of the uncoated and Nafion-coated 3D(micro/nano) V2O5/M-PSi, which was 24.6 µV/s (pH10), 8.2 µV/s (pH7) and 10.6 µV/sec (pH4) for the uncoated and dropped to 0.16 µV/s (pH7), 0.008 µV/sec (pH7) and 0.13 µV/sec for (pH10) for the Nafion-coated membrane. The present Nafion will prevent change in the oxidation number of vanadium or deterioration of the sensing membrane, which may lead to variable ion sensing qualities and erroneous pH-sensing responses as a result of the films' exposure to many ion concentrations during measurements.Even while overlaying the sensing gate of a pH EG-FETs device with a thin membrane of Nafion diminished its sensitivity, it modified the linearity in the linear and saturation areas and decreased hysteresis with even less drift. Additionally, one of the disadvantages of a Metal Oxide (MOx)-based pH sensors, linked to its sensitivity to redox species in the solution, which results to inaccurate pH measurements, may be eliminated. In contrast, due to the interference of lipids and proteins, the use of metal oxide pH-electrodes, which may adsorb on the sensor surface and impede measurements, is prohibited. This is particularly significant for the capacity to monitor pH in complex matrices, such as blood or food samples. This limitation may be avoided by covering the pH sensitive section with a semipermeable layer which allows only hydrogen ions to pass, so preserving the sensor surface from contaminating [14,15]. Since Nafion is resistant to both alkaline and acidic solutions, the device will last longer and the pH EG-FET sensor's measuring range will change or extend.ConclusionIn biosensors, the response of samples as a function of time is critical. Thus, a protective layer of Nafion selective proton permeable was deposited over the sensing gate of 3D(micro/nano)V2O5by drop casting of 10 µL of the 5% by weight Nafion solution, with the aim of boosting the long-term stability of the 3D(micro/nano)V2O5EG-FET pH-sensor. The findings demonstrated that XRD revealed the amorphous nature of the synthesized Nafion-coated membrane, while FE-SEM and EDX suggested the creation of a thick, smooth, and crack-free layer of Nafion that had penetrated into the pores. The Nafion-coated sensor's sensitivity decreased to 74.8 mV/pH, hysteresis decreased to 5.3 mV, and the sensor drift (0.16 µV/sec, 0.008 µV/sec and 0.13 µV/sec for pH10, pH7, and pH4, respectively). Compared to the coated sensor, the uncoated sensor had a sensitivity of 91.1 mV/pH, a hysteresis of 11 mV, and drift values of (24.6 µV/sec, 8.2 µV/sec, and 10.6 µV/sec for pH10, pH7, and pH4, respectively.
